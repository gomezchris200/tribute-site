Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee

The Father of the World Wide Web

About

Tim Berners-Lee is an English computer scientist most known for being the inventor of the World Wide Web. Berners-Lee first proposed an information management system on 12 March 1989, then implimented the first succesful communication between a Hypertext Transfer Protocol(HTTP) client and server via the Internet in mid-November. Berners-Lee is the director of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), which oversees the continued deveolpment of the Web. He co-founded (with his then wife-to-be Rosemary Leith) the World Wide Web Foundation. He is also a Professorial Fellow of Computer Science at the University of Oxford and a professor at the Massachusetts institute of Technology (MIT).

Awards

  • Turing Award (2016)
  • Queen Elizabeth Prize (2013)
  • Foreign Associate of the National Academy of Sciences(2009)
  • Order of Merit(2007)
  • ACM Software System Award(1995)
